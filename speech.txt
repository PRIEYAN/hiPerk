hiPerk — 2-Minute Demo Video Script
====================================
Total target: ~2:00. Word count is paced for a calm, clear delivery (~150 wpm).
Lines in [brackets] are screen/action cues, not spoken.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 1 — THE PITCH  (0:00 – 0:25)  [talking head or logo on screen]
--------------------------------------------------------------------

"Hi — this is hiPerk.

It pays open-source contributors for work that provably happened,
while revealing nothing about who did it.

You prove that your pull request was merged into a repo — and you get
paid in crypto on Stellar — without exposing your GitHub identity, and
without paying a single network fee."


--------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 2 — THE TECH  (0:25 – 1:05)  [architecture diagram / code peeks]
--------------------------------------------------------------------

"Four technologies make that possible.

First — a zero-knowledge proof, built with the RISC Zero zkVM. The proof
takes your merge notification and a private secret, runs them inside the
zkVM, and outputs only two things: an anonymous commitment, and a
one-time nullifier. Your email, your identity, your secret — none of it
ever leaves the proof boundary.

Second — Stellar and Soroban. Two Rust smart contracts: a Gatekeeper that
registers your anonymous membership, and a Perk contract that holds the
reward pool and pays you out. A fee-bump relayer sponsors every
transaction, so the contributor pays zero gas.

Third — the nullifier is marked spent on-chain, so the same merge can
never be claimed twice — and the contract still never learns who you are.

And fourth — the part that makes it hands-off — Groq."


--------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 3 — THE GROQ + AUTOMATION STORY  (1:05 – 1:30)  [dashboard]
--------------------------------------------------------------------

"When a moderator creates a module, they set a reward pool — and that
amount is escrowed on-chain the moment they click create.

From there it's fully automated. When a contributor's merge is verified,
we send the pull request's complexity — the size, the files changed — to
the Groq API. Groq scores how significant the work is, and we pay out a
small, fair slice of the pool automatically. The bigger the contribution,
the bigger the reward. No manual approval, no haggling.

And when the pool runs dry, the module closes itself."


--------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 4 — LIVE DEMO  (1:30 – 1:55)  [screen recording of the app]
--------------------------------------------------------------------

[Create a module with a reward pool]
"So here I'm a moderator. I create a module for my repo and set a
thousand-token reward pool. Click create — and it's escrowed."

[Switch to developer, submit proof, verify]
"Now as a developer, I pick the module, point to my merged pull request,
and verify it."

[Click generate proof — payout happens]
"I generate my proof — and there it is. Groq scored the PR, the perk was
calculated, and I was paid instantly. The pool balance drops by exactly
that amount."


--------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 5 — CLOSE  (1:55 – 2:00)  [logo]
--------------------------------------------------------------------

"hiPerk. Prove the work, not the worker. Thanks for watching."


====================================================================
DELIVERY NOTES
--------------------------------------------------------------------
- Total spoken words ≈ 300, which lands right around 2:00 at a natural pace.
- If you run long, cut PART 3's last line ("And when the pool runs dry…")
  and the middle of PART 2's third paragraph.
- Emphasize the four "First / Second / Third / Fourth" beats — they give
  the video a spine and make the tech easy to follow.
- Say "Groq" as "grock" (rhymes with "rock"), not "gee-roq".
- Honesty note for judges if asked: in this demo build the proof, x402

  can fail mid-demo — the create → verify → Groq-sizing → payout → close
